Salapify Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 23, 2026

The short version: your financial data stays on your device. Salapify has no servers, no accounts, no analytics, no trackers, and no ads. We cannot see your data even if we wanted to. The only copies of your financial data that ever leave your phone are the backups and exports you set up yourself, to a place you choose. The app also asks a public exchange rate service for daily rates when you use a currency feature (logging an expense in a foreign currency, or the currency converter tool), sending only your main currency code, never your data (see Live exchange rates below).

What data Salapify handles

Everything you enter in Salapify (accounts, balances, transactions, debts, people who owe you, people you owe, goals, notes, recurring items, and settings) is stored only in the app's private storage on your own device. The app itself never sends your data to us or to any server of ours. The only copies that ever leave the app's private storage are the backups and exports you set up yourself, to a place you choose (see Backups below).

Because your data lives only on your device, you decide how long it is kept. Deleting an item removes it, and uninstalling Salapify removes all of it from your phone. We never hold a copy, so there is nothing for you to request from us and nothing for us to delete on your behalf.

What we collect

Nothing. Salapify has no user accounts, no sign in, no analytics libraries, no crash reporters, and no advertising identifiers. We do not collect personal information of any kind.

Permissions the app asks for, and why

  • Notifications: only if you turn on reminders (payday, bill due dates, collecting money owed to you, or the daily logging nudge). All reminders are scheduled locally on your phone.
  • Biometrics (fingerprint or face): only if you turn on App lock. Authentication happens entirely through your phone's own system. Salapify never sees, stores, or has access to your biometric data.
  • Internet: used for two things only, both described below: downloading app updates, and fetching today's exchange rates when you use a currency feature. Your financial data is never part of either request.
  • Storage access via the system file picker: only when you choose to back up, export, restore, or import your data (including CSV import). You pick the file or destination, and Salapify reads or writes only that one file. It never browses your device on its own.

Backups are in your hands

Backups and exports are always something you start yourself. When you create a backup or a CSV export, that file is yours: you choose where it goes (your Downloads folder, your own cloud drive, a chat to yourself). Salapify has no automatic or background backup, so a copy of your data leaves the app only at the moment you make one. Treat backup files with care, since they contain your financial records in readable form, including names and phone numbers you saved for people who owe you or people you owe.

When you restore or import a file, the app keeps one temporary copy of your previous data on the device as a safety net against a bad restore, so a mistaken import can be undone. That copy also stays only on your device. Using Start fresh (erase everything) in the app deletes your data, that safety copy, and the cached exchange rates, all of it, permanently.

Update delivery

The app checks Shorebird's update service to download improvements to the app's own code. This is a standard, anonymous code update mechanism, the same kind used by many Flutter and React Native apps: it fetches only the app's own updated code, and your financial data is never part of these requests.

Live exchange rates

When, and only when, you use a currency feature (logging an expense in a currency other than your main one, or the currency converter tool), Salapify asks a public exchange rate service (open.er-api.com) for today's rates so it can convert for you. The only thing sent is the three letter code of your main currency, for example PHP. No account, no transaction, no balance, no name, and no personal information is ever part of this request. As with any app that connects to the internet, the rate service can see your device's internet (IP) address, which it needs to answer the request; it does not receive anything that identifies you or your finances. Rates are provided by Exchange Rate API (exchangerate-api.com). If you are offline or the service cannot be reached, you simply type the rate yourself, exactly as before. The rates that come back are stored only on your device so the app does not need to ask again for several hours, and they are not part of your backups.

Children

Salapify is a general audience budgeting tool and does not knowingly collect any data from anyone, children included, because it does not collect data at all.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes (for example, if an optional cloud sync feature is added someday), the change will be described here and the app will ask before anything leaves your device. On device and private stays the default, always.

Who is responsible, and how to reach us

Salapify is built and maintained by the Salapify developer, who is the party responsible for this app under the Philippine Data Privacy Act. Because the app collects nothing and keeps no copy of your data, there is no data of yours for us to access, correct, or delete. For any privacy question, open an issue on the Salapify GitHub repository, or reach the developer by email at the address shown on the app's Google Play listing.
